About

About Me

I am a PhD candidate at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen, working in the field of educational psychology and early childhood education. My research focuses on kindergarten process quality, family engagement, young children’s school readiness.

我是一名以教育理想为毕生信念的研究者与践行者。游走于中西教育语境， 深耕于教学一线与学术研究，始终致力于推动教育实践革新与教育质量提升。

自悉尼、墨尔本启程，辗转新加坡，最终回归故土；从中学教育、成人教育到学前教育， 我始终追寻优质教育的理想之光。于 Kindy Lab，我找到了将初心与使命落于实践的价值与力量。 每一次观察访谈、每一轮测评实践、每一份反馈建议，都让我愈发坚定初心： 携手同行，做有温度的研究，办有价值的教育。

Research

Research Interest

My current work is situated at the intersection of early childhood education quality, child development, and family systems.

01

Educational Quality and Evaluation

Early childhood education process quality, classroom observation, quality monitoring, evaluation tools, and evidence-informed feedback for kindergartens.

02

Child Development

Young children’s school readiness, emergent literacy and numeracy, executive function, and social-emotional development in preschool contexts.

03

Family Involvement

Family engagement, home learning environments, caregiving arrangements, and family–school partnerships in early childhood development.

Projects

Selected Research & Social Projects

Selected projects reflect my work on longitudinal child development, kindergarten process quality, citywide quality monitoring, and rural early childhood education improvement.

2023–Present · Guangdong Provincial Philosophy and Social Science Project

Longitudinal Research on the Process Quality of Kindergartens in Shenzhen and Its Impact on Child Development

A continuing longitudinal study in Shenzhen involving three years and four waves of data collection. The project examines developmental trajectories of children aged 3–6 and the school- and family-level factors associated with their development. The project includes 44 kindergartens, 135 classrooms, and 2,084 participating children and parents.

Role: Main Manager

Completed · Shenzhen Education Science Planning Project

Process Quality Characteristics of Kindergartens in Shenzhen and Their Impact on Child Development

This study examined process quality in Shenzhen kindergartens, its influencing factors, and its immediate and long-term associations with child development. The project included 24 kindergartens, 96 classrooms, and 576 participating children.

Role: Main Member

2026 · Shenzhen Municipal Quality Monitoring

2026 Shenzhen Early Childhood Education Quality Monitoring Project

A citywide preschool education quality monitoring project commissioned by Shenzhen education authorities. The project covers 10 administrative districts and 195 sampled kindergartens and is listed as a key task of the Shenzhen municipal government.

Role: Main Member; full-time quality monitor and training support

Bijie Training · China Development Research Foundation

One Village One Kindergarten: Quality Improvement

A rural early childhood education quality improvement project in Bijie, supporting fieldwork, data collection, observation training, and onsite implementation for quality improvement evaluation.

Role: Main Member; fieldwork and training support
